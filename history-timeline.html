Our History

Documenting the rise of LGBTQ+ political power in the Rio Grande Valley. From secret house meetings to sweeping the ballot box.

2015

The Spark

Following the historic Obergefell v. Hodges supreme court decision, local activists realized the need to translate legal victory into political infrastructure. A small group of 8 community members hold the first RGV Stonewall organizing meeting in a living room in Weslaco.

2017

Chartering the Chapter

Officially chartered by the Texas Stonewall Democrats. RGV Stonewall attends its first State Convention in San Antonio, sending a delegation of 5 voting members.

2019

First Major Endorsements

The PAC executes its very first questionnaire and endorsement process for local municipal races. Two endorsed city commissioners win their races, marking the first time Stonewall influences local margins directly.

2023

Defensive Action

During a grueling legislative session, RGV Stonewall charters buses taking over 100 Valley residents to Austin to testify against anti-drag and anti-trans healthcare bills. Membership surges past 300 dues-paying members.

2026

Looking Forward

Today, RGV Stonewall stands as one of the most powerful minority political caucuses in South Texas, with a dedicated student scholarship fund, an aggressive digital organizing strategy, and a goal to endorse up and down the ballot.
